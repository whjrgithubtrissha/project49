Interesting Facts About Chameleons

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder.
  • Changing skin colour is an important part of communication among chameleons.
  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Interesting Facts About Pigeons.

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  2. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  3. Pigeons have excellent hearing abilites.

Interesting Facts About Camels.

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals cannot eat.
  • Camels can completely shut thir nostrils during sandstorms.

Interesting Facts About Teddy Bears.

  1. Winnie the Pooh was based on a real bear named Winnipeg.
  2. The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the ter 'teddy bear' back to 1906.
  3. Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.

Interesting Facts About Pandas.

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas go from pink, to white, to black (or brown).
  • An adult panda can eat 12-38 kilos of bamboo per day.

Interesting facts about cicadas.

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs. They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging.
  2. Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes. Keep on the lookout.
  3. They improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil. When they die, their rotting bodies put nitrogen into the soil.
